Common Diseases in Cattle

1. Phenylketonuria (PKU)

Phenylketonuria (PKU) is a hereditary metabolic disorder that arises from a deficiency in the enzyme phenylalanine hydroxylase (PAH), which is essential for the conversion of the amino acid phenylalanine into tyrosine. When the PAH enzyme is lacking or dysfunctional, phenylalanine accumulates in the blood, leading to potentially devastating neurological consequences if not managed properly.

  • Symptoms: Cognitive impairments, developmental delays, seizures, behavioral issues, and skin disorders.
  • Management: Lifelong dietary regimen focusing on low-phenylalanine foods.

2. Lactose Intolerance

Lactose intolerance is a prevalent condition caused by a deficiency of lactase, the enzyme responsible for breaking down lactose, the sugar found in milk and dairy products. Symptoms include gas, bloating, diarrhea, and abdominal pain after consuming lactose-containing foods.

  • Symptoms: Bloating, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.
  • Management: Dietary modifications, lactose-free products, and lactase enzyme supplements.

3. Galactosemia

Galactosemia is a rare genetic disorder characterized by a deficiency in an enzyme necessary for metabolizing galactose, leading to its accumulation and serious health complications if not properly managed.

  • Symptoms: Jaundice, vomiting, poor feeding, and lethargy in infants.
  • Management: Lifelong galactose-free diet and regular monitoring.

4. Glycogen Storage Diseases (GSD)

Glycogen storage diseases (GSD) encompass a group of inherited metabolic disorders resulting from deficiencies in enzymes responsible for the synthesis or breakdown of glycogen, leading to various symptoms and complications.

  • Symptoms: Muscle cramps, fatigue, liver enlargement, and delayed development .
  • Management: Dietary modifications, enzyme replacement therapy, and organ transplantation in severe cases.
